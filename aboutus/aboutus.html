About Us


Gokak Power & Energy Limited

(A subsidiary company of Gokak Textiles)


Gokak Power and Energy Limited(GPEL) is a subsidiary of Gokak Textiles Limited. It is one of the oldest hydroelectric power generating plants in the continent and proudly the first plant to generate hydroelectric power in Asia. Situated amidst nature’s marvels , the plant dates back to the year 1887. On the 5th of October,1887 Mr. Alexander Henry Campbell commissioned the first power house under the name Gokak Water Power and Manufacturing Company. It was established with the help of a Pelton wheel, harnessing the rich source of energy from the waterfall extending from the river Ghataprabha in the town of Gokak, well known as Gokak Falls. This plant was established for the purpose of running the cotton mills which overlooked the falls . To achieve this, a canal parallel to the river was constructed and a portion of water from the river was diverted to the canal situated at a head of around fifty metres above the falls.

Presently, the plant is generating a total of 6.3 MW of power from its two hydroelectric power stations. The powerhouse situated near the mill generates 3.5 MW of power while the latest D.J.Madan Powerhouse which was commissioned in 1996, generates 2.8 MW of power.
